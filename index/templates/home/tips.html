Tips & Tricks

  • A nice way to work on decreasing the size of your Backlog is to pick two games that you like and alternate between them. This way, when you're feeling burnt out on one of the games you have something else to play whilst still working on reducing your backlog. It's win-win!

  • Be careful not to start too many games at once though, that's how Backlogs get started in the first place.

  • Remember that even though a Backlog tends to be seen and thought of as something negative, it's usually not worth sacrificing your sanity just to decrease a number. At the end of the day, playing games should be about having fun above anything else.

  • This also applies if you're an avid games collector, a game you've bought but haven't started is technically part of your backlog, but it'd be such a shame to take the plastic off that limited edition release and depreciate its value, wouldn't it?

  • If you barely have time to play as it is, whether it be because of school or your job, maybe you don't need to buy the game right now. You might even save some money. Now that's a pro gamer move!
